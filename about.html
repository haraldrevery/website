Harald Revery - About

Music from the forests and the mountains

Harald Revery is a contemporary electronic music producer based in Sweden. In an era of digital slop and cheap sample packs, Harald draws inspiration from the natural world, incorporating field-recorded elements ranging from nocturnal forest ambiences to the raw beauty of the world's landscapes. From the haunting echoes of the woods at night to the winds at the highest mountain peaks of Scandinavia, Harald captures these elements and translates them for the dancefloor.

With earth's varying wilderness and its sonics, Harald brings a unique sound experience to his sets. His dedication for making music that can ignite that special spark within people is well known to those around him. He brings the raw spirit of Europe's nature into his productions for the world to experience. With a decade of experience crafting tracks for the dancefloor, Harald knows how to get the rave experience anywhere — whether they are in a dimly lit basement bar or at a major festival. His music always hits home for those who hear it.

Logo

The letter "H" is derived from the first letter of his birth name, Harald. The split in the upper-left branch of the "H" is a highly abstracted representation of a boat's sails. It reflects Harald’s philosophy on life: you cannot go against the wind, but you can steer and move with it. Just as one cannot go against nature, only design and make choices around it.

"Revery" means "daydreamer" ("dagdrömmare" in Swedish), a recurrent name recalled by teachers in his youth. When it came to pick something to release music with, it felt like a natural fit for the atmospheric nature of his genre of music.

Hardware: Main setup is Yamaha HS7 with Native Instruments Komplete Audio 6 and a Hitachi AX-M76E system for reference listening. I have a Focusrite Scarlett 2i2 for another set of HS7 when not in my studio at home. For field-recordings I use Zoom H8.

Software: FL Studio is my main DAW (unfortunately). For synths I use Harmor (built into FL Studio), Native Instruments Rounds, Xfer Serum, Surge, Odin 2 and Arminator2 (got a softspot for jankware). For making kicks I use Sonic Academy Kick 2, but I also use Geonkick. My go to equalizer is ReaPlugs ReaEQ, since I just want good performance and no delays with routing automation, it just works. For LFO volume stuff I use Gross Beat or TiagoLr GATE-12. For reverb I use ValhallaSupermassive if I want to save CPU or Native Instruments Raum if I want to be fancy. For delay effects I just use the Fruity Delay 3.
